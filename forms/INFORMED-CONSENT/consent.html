This consent form, a copy of which will be left with you for your records and reference,
is only part of the process of informed consent. It should give you the basic idea of
what the research is about and what your participation will involve. If you would like
more detail about something mentioned here, or information not included here, you
should ask. Please take the time to read this carefully and to understand any
accompanying information.
Purpose: This experiment examines your ability to remember words. Your
performance will be compared to computational models of semantic memory. This is
done so in an effort to assess if they are descriptive models of human behavior.
Procedure: In this experiment, you will be presented with a list of words, one at a time.
Afterward, you will be shown a set of words, some of which you will have studied in the
list, and some of which will be new. Your task will be to indicate whether each word did
or did not appear in the study phase. All answers will be collected by the computer.
Risks and benefits: There are typically no risks involved, greater than that which you
might experience in the normal conduct of your everyday. If you become tired, you
should take a break. You are free to discontinue participation at any time and with no
consequences. You will benefit from participating by gaining experience with scientific
research in psychology.
Your privacy: Digital data files will be anonymous and kept indefinitely on a password
protected laboratory computer. Hard copies of your data files will be anonymous and
kept indefinitely in a file cabinet in the Cognitive Science laboratory at the University of
Manitoba. This consent form will be kept in a cabinet in the Cognitive Science laboratory
at the University of Manitoba. It will be destroyed on April 1, 2022.
Compensation: You will receive 1 credit toward your final grade in Introductory
Psychology (Psychology 1200).
Voluntary withdrawal: Even after you consent to participate in the experiment, you
can choose to withdraw your participation at any time and with no consequence. If you
choose to withdraw from the experiment, please let the experimenter know. You will be
debriefed and then you are free to leave the experiment without penalty. If after
withdrawing you would like your data to be deleted, the researcher must be informed
immediately as there will be no identifying information linked to your responses after you
leave the room.
Feedback: Following the experiment, you will be informed of the experiment’s aims and
you will be told the expected results. You will also be given a description that explains
the experiment in writing upon finishing. You can learn more about this line of work
from our laboratory webpage located at home.cc.umanitoba.ca/~jamiesor.
Dissemination: The results of the study will be presented at academic conferences and
published in academic journals. Results will also be part of a thesis.
Consent: If you wish to participate in the experiment please provide your name,
signature and the date below.
